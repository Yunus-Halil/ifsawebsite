Stage One

Future Explorers

Ages 5–7

The earliest stage of IFISA introduces learners to the wonder of the world through discovery, storytelling, unplugged computation, and hands-on exploration. Learning is play-based and curiosity-driven.

What Students Learn

Laying the Foundations of a Scientific Mind

  • Discovery and observation- noticing patterns, asking questions, making predictions
  • Unplugged coding- sequencing, algorithms, and logic without screens
  • Storytelling with technology- combining narrative with simple digital tools
  • Early robotics- programmable toys, directional movement, cause and effect
  • Creative mathematics- shapes, patterns, sorting, and spatial reasoning
  • Nature-based observation- environmental science through direct experience

Stage Milestone

Students complete a guided exploration project, demonstrating their ability to observe, document, and present a question about the world around them.

What Students Learn

Building Real Skills and Systematic Thinking

  • Coding foundations- block-based and intro text-based programming
  • Engineering design- design-build-test cycles with real materials
  • Mathematical modelling- applied maths for measurement, estimation, and problem solving
  • Data exploration- collecting, graphing, and interpreting real data sets
  • AI concepts- what AI is, how it learns, and where it affects daily life
  • Environmental systems- ecology, sustainability, and systems thinking

Stage Milestone

Students complete a design challenge, build a working prototype, and present their process and findings: the first entry in their IFISA Innovation Portfolio.

2

Stage Two

STEM Discoverers

Ages 8–11

Students begin building real technical skills alongside deepening scientific understanding. Coding, data, and design thinking become consistent parts of the weekly learning experience.

3

Stage Three

Emerging Innovators

Ages 12–14

Students cross from exploration into serious technical work. They write real code, build complex systems, engage with AI, and tackle authentic engineering challenges with increasing independence.

What Students Learn

Technical Depth and Independent Problem Solving

  • Python programming- text-based coding for real data and automation tasks
  • Robotics engineering- sensors, actuators, programming, and system integration
  • Electronics- circuits, microcontrollers, and hardware interaction
  • Design thinking- human-centered problem solving and prototyping
  • Data literacy- statistics, visualisation, and data storytelling
  • AI algorithms- how machine learning works and how to critique AI systems
  • Technical documentation- communicating processes and findings in writing

Stage Milestone

Students complete an independent technical project: a coded solution, a physical prototype, or a data investigation: documented and presented as a portfolio capstone.

Stage Four · Ages 15–18

Global STEM Scholars

The final stage of IFISA. Students choose a specialisation pathway and pursue advanced, independent, real-world work that prepares them for university and beyond.
