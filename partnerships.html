Who We Partner With

Four Partnership Tracks

IFISA brings together a diverse ecosystem of partners, each contributing uniquely to the mission of future-ready education at scale.

Schools and School Networks

Primary and secondary schools, multi-academy trusts, and school networks seeking to implement IFISA as their STEM and AI baccalaureate framework. Partner schools gain curriculum access, training, and a path to formal accreditation.

School Partnership

Universities and Research Institutions

Academic institutions that want to collaborate on curriculum research, validate IFISA learning outcomes, co-design pathways to university admission, and participate in the IFISA research and impact agenda.

Research Partnership

Technology and Industry Partners

Technology companies, startups, and industry organisations that want to contribute tools, mentors, real-world problem sets, internship pathways, and curriculum expertise to the IFISA learning ecosystem.

Industry Partnership

Philanthropic and Development Partners

Foundations, international development organisations, and impact investors who want to fund IFISA's expansion into under-resourced and emerging contexts, ensuring equity of access to future-ready education.

Funding Partnership

Partnership Value

What IFISA Partners Gain

Global Reach

Access to a growing international network of schools, students, and educators across multiple countries and education systems.

Research Access

Participation in IFISA's research and impact programme: generating evidence on what works in future-ready STEM education.

Co-Branding

Recognition as an IFISA ecosystem partner: with joint communications, event presence, and shared impact storytelling.

Talent Pipeline

Early connection with technically fluent, portfolio-bearing graduates who are ready for advanced study or industry roles.

Curriculum Integration

Opportunity to embed partner tools, platforms, and real-world problems into the IFISA curriculum and student experience.

Social Impact

Demonstrable contribution to educational equity, STEM participation, and the next generation of innovators and scientists.
