For Families

Is IFISA Right for Your Child?

IFISA is designed for learners who are curious, creative, and ready to engage deeply with the world around them. Whether your child loves building, asking questions, solving puzzles, experimenting, coding, drawing, or inventing, IFISA offers a pathway for growth.

You don't need a prodigy or a child who already loves computers. You need a child who is willing to try, fail, and try again: and a school that creates the conditions for that kind of learning.

What IFISA Gives Your Child

  • A structured, 13-year pathway of STEM and AI learning
  • Real technical skills: coding, data, engineering, robotics
  • An Innovation Portfolio that follows them to university
  • Credentials that sit alongside national qualifications
  • Confidence, agency, and a sense of themselves as innovators
  • Access to competitions, mentors, and industry connections

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Does IFISA replace our national curriculum? +

No. IFISA runs alongside your national curriculum as a second, parallel track. Students continue to study for all their national qualifications and examinations: IFISA simply adds a structured, credentialled STEM and AI pathway on top.

What age can students start IFISA? +

IFISA is designed to begin at age 5 (Stage 1) and run through to age 18 (Stage 4). However, schools can also implement IFISA starting at a later stage: beginning with Stage 2 or Stage 3: with appropriate bridging activities for students who join the pathway later.

Do students need prior coding or STEM experience? +

No. IFISA is designed to develop STEM and AI competencies from scratch: starting with unplugged, play-based activities in Stage 1. The framework assumes no prior experience and builds systematically from the earliest years.

Are IFISA credentials internationally recognised? +

IFISA is actively working toward formal international recognition for its Diploma and Certificate credentials. In the interim, IFISA portfolios and certificates are designed to be legible and compelling to universities and employers globally. Recognition is expanding as the IFISA network grows.

How do we get started as a school? +

The first step is a discovery call with our partnerships team. We'll learn about your school, your goals, your student population, and your current STEM provision: then we'll propose a staged implementation plan that fits your context and resources.
