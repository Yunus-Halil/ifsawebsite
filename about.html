Our Origin

A Response to a Global Educational Challenge

Intelligent Futures International STEM Academy (IFISA) is an international STEM and AI baccalaureate framework designed to help schools reimagine what it means to prepare young people for the future.

IFISA was created in response to a persistent global challenge: while the world has changed dramatically: reshaped by artificial intelligence, automation, data, and interconnected systems: school structures have often remained fragmented in how they approach STEM, engineering, AI, and emerging technologies.

IFISA offers a rigorous, integrated, long-term model that combines academic excellence with future-ready innovation. It is designed not as an add-on, but as a complete parallel baccalaureate that runs inside and alongside a school's existing academic programme.

Our Promise

We help schools build learning environments where students investigate, build, test, analyse, design, and communicate.

Every IFISA learning experience is anchored in real problems, structured processes, and meaningful outcomes: not just content delivery.

Vision

Building a generation that shapes the future

To build a generation of learners who are technically and scientifically grounded, computationally fluent, ethically responsible, and prepared to shape the future through innovation, leadership, and human-centered problem solving.

Mission

Delivering future-ready education at scale

To help schools deliver a future-ready STEM and AI baccalaureate programme from early years through secondary education, integrating computational thinking, engineering, data, mathematics, science, ethics, and innovation to create a pathway that prepares students for university, workforce, and lifelong innovation.

Clarity of Purpose

What IFISA Is: and Is Not

IFISA Is

  • A full baccalaureate framework: not an enrichment programme
  • A 13-year developmental pathway with cumulative outcomes
  • A school-embedded model that runs alongside national curricula
  • Credential-bearing: IFISA Diploma, certificates, microcredentials
  • Rigorous, evidence-informed, and internationally aligned
  • Scalable from a single school to a national network

IFISA Is Not

  • An after-school coding club or enrichment session
  • A one-off competition or short-term programme
  • A replacement for national academic qualifications
  • A standalone technology product or app
  • A rigid template that ignores local context
  • Reserved only for students with prior STEM experience
