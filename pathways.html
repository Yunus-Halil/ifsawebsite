IFISA's curriculum weaves seven integrated strands through all 13 years, culminating in four deep specialisation pathways for upper secondary students.

Curriculum Strands

Seven Strands Woven Across All 13 Years

These strands are not separate subjects: they are integrated dimensions of learning that run through every stage, every discipline, and every project.

Computational Thinking and Coding

Algorithms, decomposition, pattern recognition, abstraction, and programming: applied across all subjects from age 5 through 18, growing from unplugged activities to production-level code.

Mathematics for Modelling and Problem Solving

Applied mathematics, statistics, quantitative reasoning, and modelling: used to solve real problems, not just pass exams.

Science and Inquiry

Empirical investigation, hypothesis testing, observation, data collection, and evidence-based reasoning across life, physical, and earth sciences.

Engineering, Robotics, and Making

Design-build-test-iterate cycles, mechanical and electronic systems, robotics, digital fabrication, and maker culture.

Data, Statistics, and AI

Data literacy, visualisation, statistical analysis, AI concepts and algorithms, machine learning foundations, and AI ethics from early years through graduation.

Design, Creativity, and Communication

Visual communication, design thinking, creative problem solving, prototyping, and the ability to communicate technical ideas clearly and compellingly.

Ethics, Citizenship, and Leadership

Digital citizenship, AI ethics, environmental responsibility, equity, social impact, and the leadership skills to drive change in complex systems.

Upper Secondary · Ages 15–18

Four Specialisation Pathways

At Stage 4, students choose a specialisation pathway that deepens their expertise and positions them for university, research, or industry entry in their chosen field.

Pathway 1

AI and Data Science

Students develop deep expertise in working with data and intelligent systems: from statistical foundations through to machine learning and responsible AI deployment.

Machine LearningAnalyticsAI EthicsData Storytelling
Pathway 2

Robotics and Engineering

Students specialise in the design, programming, and deployment of physical systems: from embedded electronics to fully autonomous robotic platforms.

Embedded SystemsAutomationMechatronicsDesign-Build-Test
Pathway 3

Biomedical and Health Technologies

Students explore the intersection of biology, data, and technology to tackle real health and life-science challenges through bioinformatics and biomedical design.

BioinformaticsHealth DataWearablesBiomedical Design
Pathway 4

Entrepreneurship and Innovation

Students learn to identify real-world problems and design scalable solutions: developing the technical, business, and leadership skills to build and pitch impactful ventures.

Venture DesignProduct DevelopmentSocial EntrepreneurshipLeadership
